In practice

What the risk picture
looks like in use.

Three situations architects encounter. Three cases where knowing the risk profile before signing changed the outcome.

Case 01 · Revision Spiral

High-end residential renovation · 8-person interior design studio

PreForma flagged high visual inspiration dependency and low decision confidence. The risk picture showed a strong likelihood of concept-phase reopening and revision accumulation beyond the contracted scope.

Guidance generated: Two-revision limit per phase, written sign-off required before concept advancement, stakeholder identification clause.

Client requested a third concept direction in week four. The studio referenced the phase-lock clause. Client signed off. Project proceeded on schedule.

Case 02 · Scope Expansion

Commercial office fit-out · 12-person architecture firm

The risk profile flagged high autonomy need and low tolerance for process constraints. Pattern consistent with clients who expand scope incrementally and resist change-of-scope billing.

Guidance generated: Explicit scope boundary language, change-of-scope billing clause, single point of contact requirement.

Client requested structural modifications in week seven. The change-of-scope clause was triggered. Additional work billed and accepted without dispute.

Case 03 · Hidden Stakeholder

Luxury new build · Solo architect

The intake flagged a decision-by-committee pattern and elevated partner involvement probability. Risk score indicated significant chance of new decision-maker introduction after concept phase.

Guidance generated: Stakeholder identification requirement before concept phase, joint sign-off protocol for all phase completions.

Client's partner requested involvement at concept review. The joint sign-off protocol was already in the contract. No restart. No renegotiation.

These cases are illustrative examples based on the types of risk patterns PreForma identifies. They represent realistic scenarios, not documented client records.

Questions

What firms typically ask.

Does my client know they're being assessed?+
The intake is framed as a standard project onboarding questionnaire. It asks about project preferences, communication style, and decision-making approach — appropriate for any professional intake process. There is no psychological language visible to your client. The risk analysis happens on the backend.
How reliable are the risk scores?+
Every report includes a confidence indicator. When intake responses produce a clear behavioral pattern, confidence is high. When responses are mixed, the report flags it and recommends validation in conversation. PreForma improves the quality of information you have before signing — it does not eliminate judgment.
Can I use this for commercial, hospitality, and developer projects?+
Yes. PreForma supports 10 project types: residential interior, architectural projects, design & build, renovation, new construction, commercial, hospitality, developer/investment, luxury, and fast-track. The scope document, risk weights, and contract clauses are calibrated to the project type selected in the intake.
Are the contract clauses legally binding?+
The contract clauses are targeted templates — they are not legal advice and PreForma is not a law firm. They are designed to be incorporated into your existing contracts with review by your attorney. They are more targeted than generic templates because they address the specific risk patterns PreForma identified in your client's profile.
What happens after my client submits the intake?+
All 6 documents are generated automatically and delivered to your email within minutes. No dashboard login required. The mobile risk dashboard is included as an HTML file that works on any device — open it in your browser before the first meeting.
My clients would never complete a questionnaire before a meeting.+
The intake is framed as a standard project onboarding form — not a test, not a survey, not a psychological assessment. It asks about project preferences and communication approach. 10 statements, 3 minutes. Most clients complete it before the first meeting without comment.
I already know which clients are going to be difficult.+
Most architects do — after the project starts. PreForma gives you that read before you sign. It also gives you the contract language and management guidance to act on it, which is the harder part.
